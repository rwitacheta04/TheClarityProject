Thus I built The Clarity Project.

Cold mailing isn’t taught in college.

Neither is academic CV building.

We’re told:

“Don’t worry about your CV. Just study.”

Until suddenly, we are worried.

Two months before summer, we realize:

We kept studying.

We kept skill-building.

We assumed the work will speak for itself.

But it didn’t.

And now you have no CV, no idea how to cold email and find yourself without an internship.

And thus you panic apply. Send spam mails. Lower your standards for a lab.

If your work isn’t visible, no one knows it exists.

You have to explain it.
Structure it.
Put it in context.
And yes — sometimes, reach out first.

But the advice around this is overwhelming:

  • Don’t be generic
  • Be polite, but don’t sound desperate
  • Apply abroad, but not too much
  • Follow every rule, but also stand out

If you follow everything, your email becomes unreadable.

If you follow nothing, you’re ignored.

What most students actually need is someone to look at THEIR approach and say:
“This works.” or “This doesn’t — here’s why.”

TCP takes out the confusion. Gets you where YOU need to go. Cuts the fluff for you.
